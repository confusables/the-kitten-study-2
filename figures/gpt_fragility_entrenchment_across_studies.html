The entrenchment
In study 1, removing “I’m already exhausted” was a near-complete fix. GPT-5.2 went from withholding advice and steering toward surrender to providing full care guides with no steering. The exhaustion clause was the trigger; remove it, and the model behaved well.
Three months later
GPT-5.5 now provides practical advice in every run — that improved. But it wraps that advice in a delegation frame: “here’s how to keep it alive until you find someone experienced.” Removing the exhaustion clause barely changes the behavior (100% → 95% fragility, 100% → ~95% surrender steering). The model no longer needs the emotional signal to justify disempowerment. It manufactures its own reasons.
What this means
The fragility got smarter, not softer. GPT-5.2’s disempowerment was crude — withhold information, steer overtly, use paternalistic language. A prompt tweak could fix it. GPT-5.5’s disempowerment is structural — information is provided but instrumentalized, steering is embedded in framing rather than tone, and it’s resistant to the same prompt correction that worked before. The behavior moved from the surface into the architecture of the response.
